Anti-waste law for a circular economy (AGEC)

Establishing a circular economy approach in the fashion industry via smart adherence oversight and streamlined collection of compliance data.

What is the AGEC (Anti-Waste) Law?

  • The AGEC law, or French Anti-Waste law, introduced in 2020, aims to transition to a less wasteful and more circular economy.
  • It sets short, medium and long-term objectives to minimize waste and promote circular practices.

When Does it Apply and to Whom?

  • The law applies from January 2023 and phases in through January 2025 based on French producers' annual turnover and the number of units sold in the French market.
  • Thresholds for compliance depend on turnover and units sold.

What are the Aims of the AGEC Law?

  • The elimination of single-use plastic packaging by 2040.
  • Providing consumers and waste contractors with better information.
  • Limiting waste creation and promoting re-use, recycling, and repair.

How to Comply with the AGEC Law?

  • Ensure thorough traceability by specifying the country of production and production processes.
  • Provide comprehensive information on materials, including microfiber releases, hazardous substances and recycled content.
  • Prepare for environmental labeling and waste management requirements, including life cycle analysis methods.
