Uyghur Forced Labor Prevention Act - UFLPA

Effective compliance and seamless chain-of-custody reporting begins with the full transparency and traceability of your textile supply chains.

What is the UFLPA?

  • The Uyghur Forced Labor Prevention Act (UFLPA) is a legislative measure effective from June 22, 2022.
  • It specifically addresses forced labor within global supply chains, focusing on products originating from Xinjiang, China, where Uyghur Muslims face forced labor.
  • The UFLPA empowers Customs and Border Patrol to seize goods and impose financial penalties on importers found in violation.

When Does it Apply and to Whom?

  • The UFLPA applies as of June 22, 2022.
  • It applies to organizations importing products(cotton specifically for fashion companies) that were produced or manufactured in Xinjiang, China.
  • The law imposes a burden of proof on importers to demonstrate ethical workforce practices within their supply chains.

What are the Aims of the UFLPA?

  • The UFLPA aims to prevent forced labor in global supply chains, particularly in Xinjiang.
  • It sets new standards for sustainable sourcing of goods by demanding robust, data-driven proof of ethical practices.
  • This legislation introduces transparency and accountability measures in industries historically reliant on cheap labor, such as fast fashion.

How to Comply with UFLPA?

  • Conduct a thorough supply chain diligence to identify immediate risks related to Xinjiang suppliers.
  • Prioritize risks using a risk matrix, considering factors like sole-sourced inventory.
  • Implement supplier protocols, including vetting, contract stipulations and codes of conduct, to prevent violations.
